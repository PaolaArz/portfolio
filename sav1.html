SAV1 · A Manchester Fanzine

A fanzine about Peter Saville, Factory Records, and the visual identity of a city, designed as a physical record sleeve.

Concept

Three parallel stories that are really one

This started as a school assignment: design a fanzine about a designer from the course content, using the aesthetic of the subject. I chose Peter Saville because I had lived in Manchester, walked its streets, and spent years telling its story, my theatricalised walking tour of the city was already built around the same cast of characters: Factory Records, The Haçienda, Joy Division, the post-industrial rebirth of a city that had given up on itself and then became the coolest place in England.

The fanzine tells three parallel stories: the history of a city, the history of a man, and the history of a visual language, and argues that the three are inseparable.

Design Decisions

Every choice came from the source material

  • Format Designed as a record sleeve: pages fold inside like a Factory Records release, referencing Saville's approach to the LP as a designed object.
  • Colour Palette Yellow, black, and white: the Factory Records palette. Industrial, minimal, confrontational.
  • Layout Diagonal type, hazard stripe motifs, full-bleed photography, drawn directly from Saville's visual language for Factory and The Haçienda.
  • Content Three interwoven narratives: the city, the man, the aesthetic, rather than a linear biography.
