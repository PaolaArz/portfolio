NGS Onboarding Program Redesign

Redesigning Field Application Specialist onboarding from a two-week face-to-face block into a blended program that cut live time by 58% while improving relevance and autonomy.

Context

A global technical team with a slow, disconnected onboarding

The Field Application Specialists (FAS) at QIAGEN needed to support customers with complex NGS and bioinformatics questions. As a Global Product Support Specialist, I was responsible for their technical training. The existing onboarding was a two-week face-to-face program that focused on sharing technical knowledge without connecting it to the tasks FAS would actually perform in their daily job. It was slow, inefficient, and didn't reflect how a global team with different backgrounds and schedules actually learns.

The Problem

Two separate gaps, both needing a redesign

Two separate gaps existed. The first was structural: the onboarding program needed a complete redesign to make it faster, more relevant, and more learner-centred. The second was a knowledge gap I identified within my own GPS team: colleagues had no bioinformatics background and had never supported panel creation, which limited their ability to answer a specific category of customer questions confidently.

The Solution

A blended redesign and a new course to fill the gap

I redesigned the onboarding program from scratch, shifting the model from a single long face-to-face block to a blended program where theoretical knowledge was covered asynchronously before live sessions, freeing face-to-face time for troubleshooting and practical tasks. I also identified the bioinformatics gap proactively and built a separate course to address it.

My Role

Both projects, end to end

I owned both projects end to end, from needs analysis and gap identification, through design, development, and delivery. My background in bioinformatics and NGS was essential: without it, I couldn't have designed training at the depth and accuracy these audiences required.

My Approach

Structured for the way the team actually learns

Onboarding redesign

The new program was built around three types of learning activities. Learners had autonomy over sequence and content — they could choose the modules relevant to their role and prior knowledge.

Absorb

Theoretical background: eLearning modules built in Adobe Captivate with narration, interactive components and assessments, recorded lab procedure videos with detailed scripts, and written instructions with links to additional resources.

Do

Practice: guided exercises, data analysis simulations, and ungraded assessments that could be retaken until the learner felt confident.

Connect

Reference tools for daily use: infographics and job aids designed to be kept and used on the job after training ended.

Bioinformatics course

A practical asynchronous course covering the knowledge GPS colleagues needed to support panel creation and handle the customer questions they previously couldn't answer.

Slides — Bioinformatics eLearning modules

Screens from the eLearning course, showing the content structure and visual design used to teach NGS analysis concepts to a non-bioinformatics audience.

Impact

Faster onboarding, more confident specialists

58% Reduction in face-to-face time
12→5 Days of live onboarding
↓ Escalation cases as team confidence grew

The face-to-face component was reduced from 12 to 5 days. Live time was freed up entirely for troubleshooting and practical tasks. The bioinformatics course gave GPS colleagues the knowledge to handle a category of customer questions independently that they had previously needed to escalate.

Escalation cases reduced as the team became more confident and capable.
