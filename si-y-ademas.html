Sí, y Además poster

A festival poster built through a structured creative process, from free association to final concept.

Concept

The empty swing already says everything

The brief asked for a festival poster built around a strong concept and clear visual hierarchy. I chose improvisation theatre, a world I know from my own theatre background. The festival name came from the core principle of improv: "yes, and" — say yes to what is offered, and add to it.

The two concepts I wanted to convey were "you are not alone" and "losing your fear", chosen over more obvious options like "have fun" or "don't say no" because they connect to deeper emotional needs that actually motivate people to show up.

Creative Process

Four ideation tools, one unexpected pivot

I worked through four ideation tools before arriving at the final image. Free association gave me the two core concepts. The Lotus Blossom method expanded them into visual associations, gears, trust falls, tightropes. Brand Sparks pushed me further: starting from unexpected objects, I arrived at the image of a tightrope walker with a parasol. Then TinEye Labs helped me define the colour palette, strong yellows and blues to capture the energy of throwing yourself into improv.

The pivot came during the moodboard phase on Pinterest. Searching for tightrope images, I landed on trapeze. The image of someone launching from a trapeze was immediately stronger, it captured both concepts at once: the fear of letting go, and the trust that someone will catch you.

But then I went further. I realised the trapeze itself, empty, no person, already said everything. The empty swing in mid-air implies the jump has already happened. Commitment without resolution. That is what improv feels like.

Design Decisions

One object, one question implied

  • Central image A swing, cropped and off-balance, mid-motion, slightly outside the frame.
  • Concept Restraint as visual metaphor. One object, one flat colour field, one question implied.
  • Colour Flat cyan and yellow, high contrast, immediate, no gradients.
  • Typography Heavy, confident, all caps at the base, anchors the movement above.
  • Hierarchy Object first, information second. The poster communicates the feeling before it communicates the facts.
